Key definitions

  • ☆ Euthanasia: From Greek 'Eu' + 'Thanatos', meaning 'good death'. It is the act of killing or permitting the death of a person who is suffering from a life-deteriorating illness.
  • ☆ Afterlife: Life after death; the belief that existence continues after physical death.
  • ☆ Environmental Sustainability: Ensuring that the demands placed on natural resources can be met without reducing capacity to allow all people and other species of animals, as well as plant life, to live well, now and in the future.
  • ☆ Evolution: The process by which different living organisms are believed to have developed from earlier less complex forms during the history of the earth.
  • ☆ Abortion: When a pregnancy is ended so that it cannot result in a baby.
  • ☆ Quality of life: The extent of which life is pleasurable and meaningful.
  • ☆ Sanctity of life: The belief that life is precious and sacred. Most religious believers say that only humans hold this status.
  • ☆ Soul: The spiritual saspect of a being; that which connects someone to God. The soul is often regarded as non-physical and as living on after physcial death, in an afterlife.


Origin of the universe

Literal Christian view

In Christianity, the Book of Genesis explains the creation of the universe, in which God spent 6 days creating the Earth and rested on the seventh. Some Christians, such as Young and Old Earth Creationists, say that this is literally true.

Liberal Christian view

However, in Liberal Christian hermeneutics, the Book of Genesis is generally understood to be metaphorical, inferring that God is the creator of all things.They do not believe the Book of Genesis is literally true and accepts scientific theories such as the Big Bang.


Islamic view

In Islam, it is believed that Allah (ﷲ) created the world in 7 days, though there is no singular Qur'anic section that focuses only on creation and is instead scattered throughout the Qur'an. Islamic hermeneutics and scholars tend to say that these periods are to be understood literally as the Qur'an is believed to be the literal words of Allah. Islam tends to teach Old Earth Creationism, the belief that the world was created over 6 long periods of time. Islam also allows for theories such as Big Bang though they are less popular.


Humanist view

Humanists, as they are by definition atheist, do not believe in creationism and instead rely on scientific theories such as the Big Bang theory to explain the origin of the universe. They believe that religious explanations are unreliable and also believe that evolution can help to understand the way tha species are related to each other rather than relying on the religious belief that the creation of life is a mystery.
